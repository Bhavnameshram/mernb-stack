    NRI/OCI=families are eligible to adopt a child with no known health problems. NRI/OCI families are treated on par with resident Indians. The current wait time for a child with no known special needs is 4+ years. Most NRI/OCI families are open to a child with minor special needs.


    Foreign:Families with no Indian heritage or Indian families that do not qualify as NRI or OCI. The reality of the program requires us to counsel foreign families to be open to considering a child with a minimum of moderate special needs, or a child over age 10. Applications from families hoping to adopt only healthy young children (unless they are NRI/OCI) will result in an indefinite wait for referral. The more open you are to both genders and more special needs, the faster a referral will happen.

    NOTE: At least one spouse must be a US Citizen to adopt a child from a foreign country. Unfortunately, Permanent Residents and those living and/or working in the USA under other types of visas do not qualify.

    Eligibility

    The ages couples or single applicants can adopt is broken down as follows: Married couples combined
    The maximum combined age for children 0 - 2 is 85
    The maximum combined age for children 2-4 is 90
    The maximum combined age for children 4-8 is 100
    The maximum combined age for children 8+ is 110

    Single women:

    The maximum age for children 0-2 is 40
    The maximum age for children 2-4 is 45
    The maximum age for children 4-8 is 50
    The maximum age for children 8+ is 55

    The age of prospective adoptive parents as on the date of complete registration to CARINGS shall be counted for deciding the age group of the child you apply for. CARA counts years, months and days.
